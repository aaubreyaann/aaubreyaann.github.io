The Quiet After the Storm

by Jane Doe

On January 7, 2020, a storm overcame Fish Haven. My family and I were camping next to the lake, and were kept awake all night by the pounding of rain on our tent. Water levels rose, flooding our tent, but we didn't dare brave the chaos ensuing outside. At 4 a.m. the downpour finally subsided to a trickle. We decided that it was time for us to make a break for the car. We gathered our soaked belongings to the best of our ability, and counted down as we unzipped the tent. On the count of 1 we dashed for the car. We both stopped dead in our tracks after 5 feet of sprinting. A giant splashing sound came from behind us. It sounded like a wave was about to overcome us. We slowly turn as we back up towards the car. We both stopped again and turned towards one another, only to being laughing histarically. That looming wave was nothing more than a celebration, as what seemed to be every fish in the lake was jumping above the surface. It was leaps of joy at the close of a long night.
